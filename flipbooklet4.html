TWO ROLES: FACILITATORS AND PARTICIPANTS

Facilitator’s role: This is a role when a person would be focused and straight to the point during the group discussion. Without being focused, the participants might not want to be focused as well and listen to the facilitator and not even contribute to the group.

There are 3 factors for the Facilitator’s role for them to achieve a good group discussions: Cognitive, Social/Emotional and Physical.
2 / 9

First factor: Cognitive

- It has 5 sub factors for the Cognitive that should be achieved for the role to be successful

1. Identify and Articulate Objectives: Make sure that all members understand the goals of the group so that they are going to be willing to contribute to the group.
2. Create an Inclusive Environment: Respect is a must. Respect all the members verbally or non-verbally. Do not exclude other members who wants to share their thoughts and do not let the others disrespect each other.


3. Plan by Asking the Right Questions: Ask something that’s connected to the topic for better discussions and to make the group more interactive.
4. Provide Direction and Maintain Focus: Focus is also a must during a discussion. You must guide the group when they are not paying attention. Do not let them lose focus.
5. Bring Closure: Keep the track. Tracking your goal for your team is very important, it allows you and your team to see what you are heading to, especially your goals.

3 / 9

Second Factor: Social/Emotional

- This is a factor where a leader must know how to feel their members emotionally and socially. The strength and weaknesses of the members should be known by the leader to make sure not to overwork the members, the work distribution must be just and at ease so that the members would not have much of pressure. Add humors if you can to make sure that everyone is engaged.

Last Factor: Physical

- The group should not be exceeded by more than 8 members inside a group. Choose an arrangement where they can see and hear everyone in the group. Make sure that everything is comfortable so that it will not distract your members.

Participant’s Role: As a participant, you should support your leader and make their job easier by participating properly since you are the most important part of the group to make the discussion fruitful and good.

Speech is a process of sending and recieving spoken messages between people and it is often used by the Speakers as a form of communication to interact with their followers at different events.

Parliamentary Procedure:

- Call to Order: Starting point of the meeting
- Roll Call of Members Present: Attendance
- Reading Minutes of the Last Meeting: Detailed report of the past meeting
- Officers and Committee Reports: If needed or part of the agenda
- Unfinished Business: Things that needs to be decided on or resolved
- New Business: Problems that can be us for a discussion
- Announcements: A reminder for the members or if something that is needed to know
- Adjournment: Ending

In speech, there are three tools

Ethos -

A Greek word meaning "character". It means getting your audience to trust you

Pathos -

A Greek word meaning "suffering and experience". It means getting to appeal to your audience emotionally

Logos -

A Greek word meaning "Thought, Word, and Speech". It means Appealing to your audience using logical arguments

(Honestly, apart from logos, these are some pretty cool Greek Mythology levels of names, but what can I expect? they originated from Greece) There are several types of speech in terms of delivery.)
